# Website Design Brief — [Your Company Name]
### Live Sound, Recording & RF Engineering Services

---

## 1. Project Overview

Design and build a professional, modern portfolio-and-business website for a **freelance sound/audio engineer and the company he has started**. The site must serve two purposes at once:

1. **Portfolio** — showcase past and ongoing event work, client/brand collaborations, gear, and certifications.
2. **Business front** — make it easy for event organizers, artists, churches, corporates, and brands to understand services, hire gear, and get in touch.

**Target audience:** event planners, artists/bands, churches and ministries, corporate clients, festival/production companies, and brands looking for audio engineering support or gear rental.

**Tone:** professional, confident, technically credible, but approachable — not overly corporate.

---

## 2. Brand Identity & Visual Style

- **Logo:** prominent in the header (navbar) and footer. Reserve a clean, high-contrast space for it — consider a subtle reveal/animation on load.
- **Color palette:** strictly **black, white, and red**, matching the existing logo.
  - Black as the dominant background/base (or white base with black/red accents — pick one direction and stay consistent).
  - Red used sparingly and deliberately: CTAs (buttons), hover states, section dividers, icons — so it reads as an accent, not noise.
- **Typography:** a bold, modern sans-serif for headings (something with strong weight for impact — e.g., Montserrat, Poppins, or similar), paired with a clean readable body font.
- **Visual style:** high-contrast photography, dramatic event/stage lighting shots, minimal clutter, generous whitespace (or "black space"). Should feel like a live-event brand, not a generic template.

---

## 3. Site Structure & Sections

### a) Hero / Landing Section
- Full-width background video or photo (you mixing/running an event) with logo overlay and a short, punchy tagline.
- Primary CTA buttons: "View Portfolio" and "Get in Touch" / "Book Now".

### b) About / Bio
- Your story, years of experience, philosophy/approach to sound engineering.
- Company mission statement (since this represents the company, not just you personally).

### c) Services Offered
Dedicated, clearly laid-out section (grid or cards) listing:
- Live Sound Engineering
- Multitrack Recording
- Monitor Engineering
- RF Engineering (wireless mic/IEM coordination)
- Sound Consultancy
- *(Consider adding: PA System Design & Installation, Mixing/Mastering, Event Audio Production Management, Training/Workshops)*

Each service card should have an icon, short description, and ideally a "Request this service" link into your contact/booking form.

### d) Portfolio / Projects (must be easily updatable)
- A running, filterable log of every project — past and future.
- Each entry: event name, date, venue, client/brand worked with, your role, photos/videos, and a short description.
- Filter/sort by: event type (concert, church service, corporate, festival), year, or client.
- Structure this as a simple CMS-style list (even a basic admin panel or a connected spreadsheet/CMS) so you can add new entries yourself without touching code.

### e) Clients & Brand Collaborations
- A logo wall / carousel of brands and clients you've worked with.
- Optionally pair with 2–3 short case studies of flagship events (challenge → setup → result).

### f) Media Gallery
- Photo and video gallery from event setups and live runs.
- Organize by event/project (tie into the Portfolio section) rather than one giant dump.
- Lightbox viewing, video previews with sound icon, mobile-optimized loading (don't let large video files slow the site down).

### g) Certifications & Training
- Visual "badge wall" or timeline of courses/certifications completed, with issuing body, date, and (where possible) a verification link or downloadable certificate.
- Designed to be easy to append as you complete new courses.

### h) Gear for Hire
- Catalogue-style listing of equipment available for rental: category (mixers, speakers, mics, IEMs, RF equipment, cabling, etc.), photos, specs, and availability/rate info (or "enquire for rates").
- Optional: simple enquiry button per item that pre-fills a rental request form.

### i) Contact
- Phone number (click-to-call on mobile), email (click-to-email), and a contact form.
- Add a **floating WhatsApp click-to-chat button** — very effective for fast client responses.
- Consider a simple map/service-area indication if you serve a specific region.

### j) Social Media
- Icon set linking to Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, etc. — placed in the header/footer and repeated near the contact section.
- Consider embedding a live Instagram feed or YouTube reel so the site always feels current without extra manual updates.

---

## 4. Extra Features to Make It Stand Out

- **Showreel video** — a 30–60 second highlight reel autoplaying (muted) in the hero section.
- **Testimonials** — short quotes/video testimonials from clients or artists you've worked with.
- **Downloadable company profile / rate card (PDF)** — for clients who want something to forward internally.
- **FAQ section** — answers common questions (turnaround time, travel for events, deposit/booking process).
- **Booking/inquiry form with event details** — date, venue, event type, estimated audience size, services needed.
- **Blog/Insights section** (optional but great for SEO) — short posts on sound engineering tips, behind-the-scenes of big events.
- **Before/after or waveform visuals** for recording work, if applicable.
- **Dark-mode-native design** — since your palette is already black/white/red, the whole site can lean into a sleek dark-theme aesthetic by default.

---

## 5. Technical Requirements

- Fully responsive (mobile-first — many clients will find you via Instagram/WhatsApp on a phone).
- Fast load times — compress video/image assets, lazy-load galleries.
- Basic SEO setup: meta titles/descriptions, alt text on images, structured data for a local business.
- Easy content management — you should be able to add new projects, certificates, and gallery items yourself without needing a developer each time (e.g., a lightweight CMS, or a simple admin dashboard).
- Analytics integration (e.g., Google Analytics) to track inquiries and traffic sources.
- SSL/secure hosting, and a contact form that doesn't end up as spam-bait.
